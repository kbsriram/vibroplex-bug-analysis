The dit paddle of the Vibroplex

This slow motion simulation at a Vibroplex keyer's paddle reveals its quirky, complex behavior. While the dah paddle acts like a simple straight key, the dit paddle interacts in surprisingly complex ways with the press and release of the dit paddle.
The core of the mechanism is the dit paddle, which is a vibrating rod fixed at one end, and a weight that is free to move at the other.
In the neutral position, a damper is placed to one side of the center of oscillation, so any movement beyond the center of oscillation is immediately stopped (ideally.)
Pressing the dit paddle rotates the vibrating rod by a small angle, causing the rod to start vibrating.
A flexible contact is placed on the rod so that a connection is made whenever the rod goes beyond the new center of oscillation.
Unlike an electronic keyer, releasing a vibroplex paddle too early can cause a "short" dit, while releasing it too late can cause an extra "short" dit.
Press the dit button to simulate pressing the dit paddle. You should hear perfectly spaced dits and spaces if you keep it pressed. Release it at various points of the oscillation to see how it affects the dit space timing.
Note: for simplicity, this simulation moves the rod rather than rotating it, but the dynamics are similar in both cases.
